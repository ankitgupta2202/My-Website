What to study, mapped to where you are

Streams and degrees explained in plain language — pick your stage below.

Science (PCM / PCB)

  • Opens engineering, medicine, architecture, research
  • Keeps the widest range of options open post-12th
  • Best if you enjoy problem-solving and lab work

Commerce

  • Leads to CA, CS, BBA, economics, business analytics
  • Good fit if you like numbers, markets, and strategy
  • Can still add Maths for finance-heavy careers

Humanities / Arts

  • Leads to law, civil services, design, journalism, psychology
  • Best for students drawn to people, society, and ideas
  • Often underrated for how wide its career map actually is

Engineering & Tech

  • B.Tech (CSE, AI/ML, ECE, Mechanical, Civil)
  • B.Sc Data Science / Statistics
  • Integrated M.Sc in core sciences

Medical & Health Sciences

  • MBBS, BDS, BAMS
  • B.Sc Nursing, B.Pharm
  • Physiotherapy (BPT), Allied Health Sciences

Commerce & Management

  • B.Com (Hons), BBA, BMS
  • CA / CS / CMA professional tracks
  • Economics (Hons)

Design & Architecture

  • B.Des (Product, UX, Fashion, Communication)
  • B.Arch
  • Animation & VFX programs

Law & Humanities

  • BA LLB (5-year integrated)
  • BA Political Science / Psychology / Journalism
  • Social Work (BSW)

Public Service Track

  • BA Public Administration
  • Any degree + UPSC/State PCS prep
  • B.Sc Environmental Science

AI, ML & Data Science

  • Fastest-growing hiring category across sectors
  • Pairs well with almost any core engineering degree

Climate & Renewable Energy

  • Environmental engineering, ESG analytics
  • Solar & EV-focused specializations

Health-Tech & Biotech

  • Biotechnology, genomics, medical devices
  • Growing research and industry demand

UX, Product & Design Tech

  • Design roles now central to every digital product
  • Strong for creatively-minded, detail-oriented students

Cybersecurity

  • Rising demand as more services move online
  • Good fit alongside a CSE or IT degree

FinTech & Business Analytics

  • Blends commerce fundamentals with data skills
  • Strong entry point into banking and startups
